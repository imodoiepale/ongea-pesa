## üéØ IP Protection Overview

### What is Intellectual Property?

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to **creations of the mind** that have commercial value and are protected by law. For Ongea Pesa, IP protection ensures:

1. **Competitive Advantage:** Prevents competitors from copying innovations
2. **Brand Protection:** Maintains brand integrity and customer trust
3. **Revenue Generation:** Enables licensing and monetization opportunities
4. **Investment Attraction:** Demonstrates value to investors and partners
5. **Legal Recourse:** Provides legal tools to stop infringement

### Types of IP Protection Relevant to Ongea Pesa

#### 1. **Trademarks** üè∑Ô∏è
- **What:** Brand names, logos, slogans, product names
- **Protection Duration:** 10 years (renewable indefinitely)
- **Registration Body:** Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI)
- **Cost:** KES 4,000 - 7,000 per class (local) | USD 150-250 (foreign)
- **Timeline:** 8-18 months

#### 2. **Copyright** üìù
- **What:** Software code, UI/UX designs, documentation, content
- **Protection Duration:** Life of author + 50 years (software: 50 years from creation)
- **Registration Body:** Kenya Copyright Board (KECOBO)
- **Cost:** KES 1,000 per work
- **Timeline:** Automatic upon creation; registration strengthens enforcement

#### 3. **Patents** üí°
- **What:** Novel technical inventions (e.g., voice authentication method)
- **Protection Duration:** 20 years from filing date
- **Registration Body:** KIPI (Kenya) | ARIPO (Regional) | WIPO PCT (International)
- **Cost:** KES 2,000 - 20,000+ (varies by stage)
- **Timeline:** 2-4 years

#### 4. **Utility Models** üîß
- **What:** Incremental technical improvements
- **Protection Duration:** 10 years
- **Registration Body:** KIPI
- **Cost:** Lower than patents
- **Timeline:** Faster than patents (6-12 months)

#### 5. **Trade Secrets** üîê
- **What:** Confidential business information, algorithms, processes
- **Protection Duration:** Indefinite (while kept secret)
- **Protection Method:** NDAs, access controls, confidentiality policies
- **Cost:** Internal management costs
- **Timeline:** Immediate

## üè¢ Legal Entity Requirements

### Prerequisites for IP Protection

Before filing IP applications, ensure Ongea Pesa has completed these foundational steps:

#### ‚úÖ Step 1: Business Registration

**Action:** Register company with Business Registration Service (BRS)

- **Platform:** [eCitizen Portal](https://brs.ecitizen.go.ke/)
- **Documents Required:**
  - Proposed company name (3 alternatives)
  - Memorandum and Articles of Association
  - Details of directors and shareholders
  - Registered office address in Kenya
- **Cost:** KES 10,950 (private limited company)
- **Timeline:** 3-7 business days
- **Output:** Certificate of Incorporation

#### ‚úÖ Step 2: Tax Registration

**Action:** Register with Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA)

- **Platform:** [iTax Portal](https://itax.kra.go.ke/)
- **Requirements:**
  - Personal Identification Number (PIN) for company
  - VAT registration (if turnover > KES 5M annually)
  - PAYE registration (if employing staff)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Timeline:** 1-3 business days
- **Output:** KRA PIN Certificate

#### ‚úÖ Step 3: Business Permits

**Action:** Obtain relevant business permits

- **County Business Permit:** From county government
- **Fintech Licenses:** Central Bank of Kenya (if applicable)
- **Data Protection Registration:** Office of the Data Protection Commissioner
- **Cost:** Varies by county and license type
- **Timeline:** 2-8 weeks

### Why This Matters for IP

- **Legal Standing:** Only registered entities can own IP in their corporate name
- **Enforcement Rights:** Courts require proof of legal entity status
- **International Protection:** Foreign IP offices require company registration proof
- **Investment & Licensing:** Investors and partners need clean corporate structure

- [ ] Pay official fees (per class)
- [ ] Monitor application status
- [ ] Respond to examiner objections (if any)
- [ ] Monitor publication in IP Journal
- [ ] Defend against oppositions (if any)
- [ ] Receive registration certificate
- [ ] Set renewal reminder (10 years)

- [ ] Identify copyrightable works (code, designs, content)
- [ ] Prepare work specimens/samples
- [ ] Complete KECOBO registration forms
- [ ] Pay registration fee (KES 1,000 per work)
- [ ] Receive copyright certificate
- [ ] Maintain records of creation dates
- [ ] Mark works with ¬© symbol and year
- [ ] Store source files securely

- [ ] Conduct prior art search
- [ ] Assess novelty and inventive step
- [ ] Prepare detailed technical description
- [ ] Draft patent claims
- [ ] Engage registered patent agent
- [ ] File patent application with KIPI
- [ ] Maintain confidentiality before filing

- [ ] Consider international filing (ARIPO/PCT)

- [ ] Identify confidential information
- [ ] Implement access controls
- [ ] Draft NDA templates (mutual & unilateral)
- [ ] Require NDAs from all employees

## üìñ Glossary

### Key Terms

**ARIPO** - African Regional Intellectual Property Organization; enables regional IP protection across 20+ African countries

**Certificate of Incorporation** - Official document proving company registration in Kenya

**Confidential Information** - Non-public business information protected as trade secret

**Copyright** - Legal protection for original creative works (software, designs, content)

**Distinctive Mark** - Trademark that uniquely identifies goods/services from one source

**Examiner** - KIPI official who reviews IP applications for compliance

**Infringement** - Unauthorized use of protected IP rights

**KECOBO** - Kenya Copyright Board; government body managing copyright

**KIPI** - Kenya Industrial Property Institute; government body managing industrial property

**Madrid Protocol** - International treaty enabling multi-country trademark registration through single application

**Nice Classification** - International system categorizing goods/services for trademark purposes (45 classes)

**NDA** - Non-Disclosure Agreement; contract protecting confidential information

**Opposition** - Formal objection to IP application by third party

**Patent** - Exclusive rights to novel invention for 20 years

**PCT** - Patent Cooperation Treaty; international patent filing system

**Prior Art** - Existing knowledge/technology relevant to patent novelty assessment

**Trade Mark (TM)** - Symbol, word, or design identifying goods/services

**Trade Secret** - Confidential business information providing competitive advantage

**TM2 Form** - Official KIPI trademark application form

**TM27 Form** - KIPI trademark preliminary search form

**Utility Model** - Protection for incremental technical improvements (10 years)

**WIPO** - World Intellectual Property Organization; UN agency managing global IP systems
