## üî¨ Introduction to Patents

### What is a Patent?

A **patent** is an exclusive right granted for an invention that provides a new technical solution to a problem. It gives the patent owner the right to prevent others from making, using, selling, or importing the invention for a limited period (20 years).

**Legal Framework in Kenya:**
- **Industrial Property Act, 2001**
- **Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI)** - regulatory authority
- **Paris Convention** - international patent treaty
- **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** - international filing system
- **Harare Protocol (ARIPO)** - regional patent system

### Key Principles

1. **Territorial Rights:** Patents are country-specific (Kenya patent only protects in Kenya)
2. **Limited Duration:** 20 years from filing date (not renewable)
3. **Disclosure Requirement:** Must publicly disclose invention details
4. **Examination:** Rigorous examination for novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability
5. **Maintenance Fees:** Annual fees required to keep patent in force

### Why Patents Matter for Fintech

**Benefits:**
- ‚úÖ **Competitive Advantage:** 20-year monopoly on innovation
- ‚úÖ **Market Exclusivity:** Prevent competitors from copying technology
- ‚úÖ **Revenue Generation:** License technology to others
- ‚úÖ **Investment Attraction:** Demonstrates innovation and IP value
- ‚úÖ **Defensive Protection:** Build patent portfolio to deter litigation

**Challenges:**
- ‚ùå **High Cost:** KES 50,000 - 500,000+ for full process
- ‚ùå **Long Timeline:** 2-4 years to grant
- ‚ùå **Public Disclosure:** Invention details become public
- ‚ùå **Software Patentability:** Limited in many jurisdictions
- ‚ùå **Maintenance Burden:** Annual fees and documentation

## üîç What Can Be Patented

### Patentability Requirements (Kenya Industrial Property Act)

An invention is patentable if it meets **ALL three criteria:**

#### 1. **Novelty**
- Must be new (not known anywhere in the world)
- Not disclosed publicly before filing date
- Not in prior art (existing patents, publications, public use)

**Test:** Would a person skilled in the art find this invention in existing knowledge?

#### 2. **Inventive Step (Non-Obviousness)**
- Not obvious to a person skilled in the art
- Involves creative leap beyond existing knowledge
- Solves problem in unexpected way

**Test:** Would a skilled person arrive at this solution through routine experimentation?

#### 3. **Industrial Applicability (Utility)**
- Can be made or used in industry
- Has practical application
- Produces useful result

**Test:** Can this invention be manufactured or used in practice?

### What CANNOT Be Patented

‚ùå **Excluded from Patentability (Kenya):**

| Category | Reason | Alternative Protection |
|----------|--------|------------------------|
| **Discoveries** | Not inventions (e.g., mathematical formula) | Trade secret |
| **Scientific Theories** | Abstract ideas | Copyright (if written) |
| **Mathematical Methods** | Not technical | Trade secret |
| **Aesthetic Creations** | Not technical | Copyright, design registration |
| **Business Methods** | Not technical (in most jurisdictions) | Trade secret |
| **Computer Programs "as such"** | Software alone not patentable | Copyright |
| **Presentation of Information** | Not technical | Copyright |
| **Medical Treatment Methods** | Public health policy | Trade secret |
| **Plant/Animal Varieties** | Separate protection regime | Plant breeders' rights |
| **Immoral/Offensive Inventions** | Public policy | None |

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical for Ongea Pesa:**

**Software Patents in Kenya:**
- ‚ùå **Pure software** (algorithm alone) = NOT patentable
- ‚úÖ **Software + technical effect** (solves technical problem) = MAY be patentable
- ‚úÖ **Software + hardware** (embedded system) = MORE likely patentable

**Business Method Patents:**
- ‚ùå **Pure business method** (e.g., "voice-activated payment concept") = NOT patentable
- ‚úÖ **Business method + technical implementation** (e.g., "voice authentication using specific AI technique") = MAY be patentable

## üá∞üá™ Kenya Patent Filing Process

### Overview

**Regulatory Body:** Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI)  
**Governing Law:** Industrial Property Act, 2001  
**System:** First-to-file  
**Duration:** 20 years from filing date  
**Examination:** Substantive examination required

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### **Step 1: Prior Art Search**

**Purpose:** Assess novelty and avoid wasting money on non-novel inventions

**How to Search:**

1. **KIPI Database:**
   - Visit: [https://kipi.go.ke/patents](https://kipi.go.ke/patents)
   - Search Kenya patent database
   - Free

2. **WIPO Patentscope:**
   - Visit: [https://patentscope.wipo.int](https://patentscope.wipo.int)
   - Search 100+ million patents worldwide
   - Free

3. **Google Patents:**
   - Visit: [https://patents.google.com](https://patents.google.com)
   - User-friendly interface
   - Free

4. **Espacenet (European Patent Office):**
   - Visit: [https://worldwide.espacenet.com](https://worldwide.espacenet.com)
   - Comprehensive global database
   - Free

**Search Strategy:**
- Use keywords: "voice authentication payment", "biometric financial transaction", "voice security"
- Search patent titles, abstracts, and claims
- Review similar patents from competitors
- Check filing dates (anything before your invention date is prior art)

**Cost:**
- DIY: Free
- Professional search: KES 50,000 - 150,000

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** Do NOT publicly disclose invention before filing (destroys novelty)

---

#### **Step 2: Engage Patent Agent**

**Why You Need a Patent Agent:**
- Patent drafting is highly technical
- Poorly drafted patent = weak protection
- Claims define scope of protection
- Agents understand KIPI procedures

**Requirements:**
- Must be registered with KIPI
- Technical + legal expertise
- Experience in relevant field (software, fintech)

**Find Registered Agents:**
- KIPI website: [https://kipi.go.ke/patents/agents](https://kipi.go.ke/patents/agents)
- Kenya Intellectual Property Agents Association

**Cost:** KES 100,000 - 300,000 (full patent drafting + filing)

**What Agent Does:**
- Conducts/reviews prior art search
- Drafts patent application
- Prepares claims (most critical part)
- Files application with KIPI
- Responds to examiner objections
- Manages prosecution until grant

---

#### **Step 3: Prepare Patent Application**

**Patent Application Components:**

**1. Request Form (Patent Form 1)**
- Applicant details (Ongea Pesa Limited)
- Inventor details (actual inventors)
- Title of invention
- Priority claim (if applicable)
- Agent details

**2. Description (Specification)**
- **Title:** Clear, concise (e.g., "Voice Biometric Authentication System for Financial Transactions")
- **Technical Field:** Area of technology (e.g., "mobile payment security")
- **Background Art:** Existing solutions and their problems
- **Summary of Invention:** What invention does and how it solves problem
- **Brief Description of Drawings:** List of figures
- **Detailed Description:** Step-by-step explanation of invention
- **Industrial Applicability:** How invention can be used in practice
- **Best Mode:** Best way to implement invention (required)

**Length:** Typically 10-50 pages

**3. Claims**
- **Independent Claims:** Broadest scope of protection
- **Dependent Claims:** Narrow specific embodiments

**Example Claim Structure:**
```
Claim 1 (Independent):
A method for authenticating financial transactions using voice biometrics, 
comprising the steps of:
  (a) receiving a voice command from a user via a mobile device;
  (b) extracting voice biometric features using an AI model;
  (c) comparing extracted features against a stored voiceprint;
  (d) calculating a similarity score;
  (e) authorizing the transaction if the score exceeds a threshold.

Claim 2 (Dependent):
The method of claim 1, wherein the AI model is a deep neural network 
trained on voice samples.

Claim 3 (Dependent):
The method of claim 1, wherein the threshold is dynamically adjusted 
based on transaction amount.
```

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical:** Claims define protection scope. Too broad = rejected. Too narrow = weak protection.

**4. Abstract**
- 150-word summary of invention
- Used for searching/indexing

**5. Drawings**
- Flowcharts, system diagrams, architecture
- Must be clear, labeled
- Black and white (color allowed but not required)

**6. Declaration of Inventorship**
- Names of actual inventors
- Inventors must be natural persons (not companies)
- Company owns patent, inventors are credited

#### **Step 5: Formality Examination**

**Timeline:** 1-3 months after filing

**What KIPI Checks:**
- Application form complete
- Fees paid
- Description, claims, abstract present
- Drawings (if referenced)
- Proper format

**Possible Outcomes:**
- ‚úÖ **Accepted:** Proceeds to publication
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Deficiency Notice:** Correct errors within 2 months
- ‚ùå **Rejected:** Application abandoned (rare at this stage)

---

#### **Step 6: Publication (18 Months)**

**Timeline:** 18 months from filing date (or priority date)

**Process:**
- KIPI publishes application in **Kenya Industrial Property Journal**
- Application becomes public (invention disclosed)
- Anyone can view application details

**Purpose:**
- Public notification
- Allows third parties to oppose (rare for patents)
- Establishes prior art for future applications

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** After publication, invention is public. Cannot withdraw and keep secret.

---

#### **Step 7: Request for Substantive Examination**

**Deadline:** Within 3 years of filing date

**Process:**
1. File request for examination (Patent Form 9)
2. Pay examination fee: KES 4,000 (local) / USD 200 (foreign)
3. KIPI assigns examiner

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical:** If you don't request examination within 3 years, application is deemed withdrawn.

**Why Delay Examination?**
- Assess commercial viability first
- Wait for international search results (if PCT filed)
- Delay costs
- Monitor competitor activity

**For Ongea Pesa:** Request examination within 6-12 months (don't delay unnecessarily)

---

#### **Step 8: Substantive Examination**

**Timeline:** 1-2 years after examination request

**What Examiner Assesses:**

1. **Novelty:**
   - Search prior art (patents, publications, public use)
   - Compare invention to prior art
   - Assess if truly new

2. **Inventive Step:**
   - Would skilled person find invention obvious?
   - Is there creative leap?
   - Does it solve problem in unexpected way?

3. **Industrial Applicability:**
   - Can invention be made/used?
   - Practical utility?

4. **Clarity and Support:**
   - Are claims clear?
   - Is description sufficient to enable person to make invention?
   - Do claims have support in description?

5. **Excluded Subject Matter:**
   - Is invention patentable subject matter?
   - Not business method, software "as such", etc.

**Possible Outcomes:**

‚úÖ **Allowance:** Patent granted (rare on first examination)

‚ö†Ô∏è **Examination Report (Objections):**
- Examiner raises objections
- Lack of novelty (prior art found)
- Lack of inventive step
- Claims too broad
- Insufficient description
- **Response Deadline:** 3 months (extendable)

‚ùå **Final Rejection:** Application refused (can appeal)

---

#### **Step 9: Respond to Examination Report**

**If Objections Raised:**

**Options:**

1. **Amend Claims:**
   - Narrow claims to overcome prior art
   - Add limitations from description
   - Most common response

2. **Argue Against Objections:**
   - Explain why prior art doesn't anticipate invention
   - Argue inventive step
   - Provide evidence/expert opinions

3. **Amend Description:**
   - Clarify technical details
   - Add missing information
   - Correct errors

4. **Combination:**
   - Amend claims + argue

**Response Preparation:**
- Work with patent agent (essential)
- Review prior art cited by examiner
- Identify key differences
- Draft amendments carefully

**Cost:** KES 30,000 - 100,000 (agent fees per response)

**Timeline:** May require multiple rounds (2-4 examination reports common)

---

#### **Step 10: Grant and Publication**

**If Examiner Satisfied:**

1. **Notice of Allowance** issued
2. Pay grant fee: KES 4,000 (local) / USD 200 (foreign)
3. Pay certificate fee: KES 2,000 (local) / USD 100 (foreign)
4. Pay 1st annual maintenance fee (due at grant)
5. KIPI issues **Patent Certificate**
6. Patent published in IP Journal

**What You Receive:**
- Patent certificate with patent number
- Exclusive rights for 20 years from filing date
- Right to sue infringers
- Right to license/assign patent

**Timeline from Filing to Grant:**
- **Fast Track:** 2 years
- **Typical:** 3-4 years
- **Slow:** 5+ years (if multiple objections)

## üîß Utility Models in Kenya

### What is a Utility Model?

**Utility Model** (also called "petty patent" or "innovation patent") provides protection for **incremental technical improvements** that don't meet full patent requirements.

**Legal Basis:** Industrial Property Act, 2001 (Part III)

### Key Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Duration** | 10 years from filing (not renewable) |
| **Examination** | Formality only (no substantive examination) |
| **Novelty** | Local/relative novelty (not worldwide) |
| **Inventive Step** | Lower threshold than patents |
| **Cost** | ~50% of patent cost |
| **Timeline** | 6-12 months to registration |
| **Scope** | Devices, apparatus, tools, processes |

### When to Use Utility Models

‚úÖ **Good for:**
- Incremental improvements to existing technology
- Minor innovations with commercial value
- When speed is important
- Budget constraints
- Local protection sufficient

‚ùå **Not Suitable for:**
- Groundbreaking inventions (use patent)
- Software/business methods (same exclusions as patents)
- International protection needed (limited recognition)

### Utility Model Filing Process

**Simplified Process:**

1. **File Application** (Utility Model Form 1)
   - Similar to patent application
   - Description, claims, drawings
   - Filing fee: KES 1,000 (local) / USD 50 (foreign)

2. **Formality Examination** (1-2 months)
   - Check completeness
   - No novelty/inventive step examination

3. **Publication** (if formalities OK)
   - Published in IP Journal
   - 60-day opposition period

4. **Registration** (if no opposition)
   - Registration fee: KES 2,000 (local) / USD 100 (foreign)
   - Certificate issued

**Total Cost:** KES 10,000 - 50,000 (including agent fees)

**Timeline:** 6-12 months

### Utility Model for Ongea Pesa

**Potential Utility Model Candidates:**

1. **Improved Voice Command UI Layout**
   - Novel button arrangement for voice activation
   - Faster than patent
   - Lower cost

2. **Voice Feedback Optimization Method**
   - Specific way of providing audio confirmations
   - Incremental improvement

3. **Offline Voice Command Queuing System**
   - Device for storing voice commands when offline
   - Practical improvement

**Recommendation:** Use utility models for **minor innovations** while pursuing patents for **major breakthroughs**.

**Member States (20):**
Botswana, Cape Verde, Eswatini, Gambia, Ghana, Kenya, Lesotho, Liberia, Malawi, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, Rwanda, S√£o Tom√© and Pr√≠ncipe, Seychelles, Sierra Leone, Somalia, Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe

### How ARIPO Patents Work

**Process:**

1. **File ARIPO Application:**
   - Via KIPI (Kenya national office) or directly with ARIPO (Harare, Zimbabwe)
   - Designate countries where protection wanted

- ‚úÖ Single application for multiple countries

- ‚úÖ Centralized filing
- ‚úÖ English language (no translations)

- ‚ùå Each country can still refuse
- ‚ùå Not all African countries are members
- ‚ùå Examination timelines vary by country

## üåê PCT International Patent System

### What is PCT?

**Patent Cooperation Treaty** - international patent filing system administered by WIPO. Enables single application to seek protection in 150+ countries.

**How PCT Works:**

**1. File International Application:**
- Via KIPI (Kenya receiving office) or directly with WIPO
- Single application in English
- Designate countries/regions

**2. International Phase (12-18 months):**
- **International Search:** WIPO searches prior art
- **International Search Report (ISR):** Lists relevant prior art
- **Written Opinion:** Preliminary patentability assessment
- **Optional:** International Preliminary Examination

**3. National/Regional Phase (30-31 months):**
- Enter national phase in each designated country
- Each country examines under local law
- Pay national fees, engage local agents
- Countries grant or refuse independently

**4. Grant:**
- If country approves, national patent granted
- 20-year protection in that country

### PCT Advantages

‚úÖ **Benefits:**
- **Delay Costs:** Postpone expensive national filings by 18-30 months
- **Assess Patentability:** Get international search results before committing
- **Market Assessment:** Evaluate commercial viability before national phase
- **Single Filing:** One application covers 150+ countries
- **Priority:** Establishes international priority date

## üõ°Ô∏è Patent Maintenance and Enforcement

### Annual Maintenance Fees

**Requirement:** Pay annual fees to keep patent in force

**Kenya Patent:**
- Due date: Anniversary of filing date
- Grace period: 6 months (with late fee)
- Late fee: 50% surcharge
- If not paid: Patent lapses (cannot be restored)

**Payment Process:**
1. KIPI sends renewal reminder (usually 3 months before)
2. Pay via eCitizen portal or bank
3. Receive payment receipt
4. Patent remains in force

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical:** Set calendar reminders! Missing payment = loss of patent rights.

**Fee Schedule:** See cost table above (increases over time)

### Patent Enforcement

**Exclusive Rights:**

As patent owner, you can prevent others from:
- ‚ùå Making the invention
- ‚ùå Using the invention
- ‚ùå Selling the invention
- ‚ùå Importing the invention
- ‚ùå Offering to sell the invention

**Without your permission** in Kenya (or designated ARIPO countries)

### Infringement Actions

**If Someone Infringes Your Patent:**

**1. Cease and Desist Letter**
- Send formal demand to stop
- Reference patent number and claims
- Demand compliance within 14-30 days
- Cost: KES 20,000-50,000

**2. Negotiated License**
- Offer to license patent to infringer
- Collect royalties instead of suing
- Cost: Negotiable

**3. Court Action (High Court)**
- File patent infringement suit
- Seek:
  - Injunction (stop infringement)
  - Damages (compensation)
  - Account of profits
  - Delivery up of infringing products
- Cost: KES 500,000 - 2,000,000+
- Timeline: 2-4 years

**Remedies:**
- **Injunction:** Court order to stop infringement
- **Damages:** Compensation for losses
- **Account of Profits:** Infringer pays profits made
- **Destruction:** Infringing products destroyed

**Criminal Penalties:**
- Fine: Up to KES 1,000,000
- Imprisonment: Up to 5 years
- Or both

### Defenses to Infringement

**Infringer May Argue:**
- Patent is invalid (lack of novelty, inventive step)
- Their product doesn't fall within claims
- Prior use (used invention before your filing date)
- Exhaustion (bought patented product legitimately)

**‚ö†Ô∏è Risk:** Court may invalidate your patent if defenses succeed

### Patent Licensing

**Generate Revenue from Patent:**

**Exclusive License:**
- Only licensee can use invention
- You cannot use or license to others
- Higher royalty rates

**Non-Exclusive License:**
- Multiple licensees allowed
- You retain right to use
- Lower royalty rates

**License Terms:**
- Royalty: % of sales or fixed fee
- Territory: Kenya, Africa, worldwide
- Duration: Term of patent or shorter
- Field of use: Specific applications

**Example Royalty Rates (Fintech):**
- 2-5% of gross revenue
- Or fixed fee: USD 10,000-100,000/year

## ‚ùì FAQs

**Q: Can software be patented in Kenya?**  
A: Software "as such" cannot be patented. However, software that produces a technical effect or solves a technical problem may be patentable.

**Q: How long does it take to get a patent in Kenya?**  
A: 3-5 years from filing to grant (if successful).

**Q: How much does a patent cost?**  
A: KES 200,000-500,000 (USD 2,000-5,000) including agent fees.

**Q: Do I need a patent agent?**  
A: Highly recommended. Patent drafting is complex and mistakes are costly.

**Q: What's the difference between a patent and copyright?**  
A: Patent protects inventions/methods. Copyright protects creative expression (code, designs).

**Q: Can I file a patent myself?**  
A: Yes, but not recommended. Poor drafting = weak/invalid patent.

**Q: What if I find prior art after filing?**  
A: Disclose to KIPI. Failure to disclose can invalidate patent.

**Q: Can I patent a business method?**  
A: Generally no, unless it has a technical implementation.

**Q: Should I file in Kenya first or PCT?**  
A: File Kenya first to establish priority, then PCT within 12 months.

**Q: What if someone infringes my patent?**  
A: Send cease and desist, negotiate license, or sue in court.

**Q: Can I sell my patent?**  
A: Yes, patents can be assigned/sold. Record assignment with KIPI.

**Q: What happens after 20 years?**  
A: Patent expires, invention enters public domain.
