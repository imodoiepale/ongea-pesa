## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:

**"Confidential Information"** means all information, whether oral, written, electronic, visual, or in any other form, disclosed by one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**"), including but not limited to:

(a) Technical information: software, source code, algorithms, designs, specifications, prototypes, know-how, processes, techniques, methods, formulas, data, research, development plans;

(b) Business information: business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer lists, supplier lists, transaction data, marketing plans, sales data, forecasts, budgets;

(c) Proprietary information: trade secrets, inventions, discoveries, improvements, intellectual property;

(d) Any information marked or identified as "Confidential", "Proprietary", or similar designation;

(e) Any information that would reasonably be considered confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

**1.2** Confidential Information shall not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

(d) Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party:
   - Gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement;
   - Cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking protective measures;
   - Discloses only the minimum information required.

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

**2.1 Confidentiality**

The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose;

(d) Protect the Confidential Information with the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or create derivative works from the Confidential Information except as necessary for the Purpose.

**2.2 Limited Disclosure**

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to:

(a) Its employees, officers, directors, consultants, advisors, and contractors (collectively "**Representatives**") who:
   - Have a legitimate need to know for the Purpose;
   - Are informed of the confidential nature of the information;
   - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement;

(b) The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

**2.3 Notification of Breach**

The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Immediately notify the Disclosing Party in writing of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information;

(b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party in remedying such breach;

(c) Take all reasonable steps to prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure.

**3.2** Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any license, right, title, or interest in or to:

(a) The Confidential Information;

(b) Any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Any patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, or other proprietary rights.

**3.3** All intellectual property rights in any work product, invention, or discovery created solely by the Receiving Party using the Confidential Information shall be subject to separate written agreement between the Parties.

## 4. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION

**4.1** Upon the earlier of:

(a) Termination of this Agreement;

(b) Completion or abandonment of the Purpose;

(c) Written request by the Disclosing Party;

The Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party's option:

(i) Return all Confidential Information (including all copies, notes, summaries, and derivatives) to the Disclosing Party; or

(ii) Destroy all Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing to the Disclosing Party.

**4.2** Notwithstanding Section 4.1, the Receiving Party may retain:

(a) One archival copy of Confidential Information solely for legal compliance purposes, provided such copy remains subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement;

(b) Confidential Information stored in automatic computer backup systems, provided such information is not accessible in the ordinary course of business and remains subject to this Agreement.

## 6. NO OBLIGATION

**6.1** This Agreement does not:

(a) Obligate either Party to disclose any particular information;

(b) Obligate either Party to enter into any business relationship or transaction;

(c) Restrict either Party from entering into similar agreements with third parties;

(d) Restrict either Party from developing, using, or marketing products or services that may be similar to those of the other Party, provided such development does not involve use of the other Party's Confidential Information.

**6.2** Neither Party shall have any obligation to the other Party except as expressly set forth in this Agreement.

## 7. REMEDIES

**7.1** The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) Breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy;

(b) The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the necessity of posting a bond;

(c) Such equitable relief shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available at law or in equity.

**7.2** In the event of breach, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to:

(a) Injunctive relief to prevent further breach;

(b) Damages for losses suffered as a result of the breach;

(c) Account of profits made by the Receiving Party from the breach;

(d) Recovery of all costs and expenses, including reasonable attorney fees, incurred in enforcing this Agreement.

**7.3** The remedies set forth in this Section 7 are cumulative and not exclusive.
