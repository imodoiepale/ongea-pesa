## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Confidential Information"** means all information, whether oral, written, electronic, visual, or in any other form, disclosed by Ongea Pesa to the Receiving Party, including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical Information:**
   - Software, source code, object code, algorithms, APIs
   - Designs, specifications, architecture, flowcharts
   - Prototypes, know-how, processes, techniques, methods
   - Research, development plans, technical documentation
   - Voice recognition technology, AI models, machine learning algorithms
   - Database schemas, data structures, encryption methods

(b) **Business Information:**
   - Business plans, strategies, models, forecasts
   - Financial information, pricing, revenue, costs, margins
   - Customer lists, user data, transaction records
   - Supplier lists, vendor agreements, partner contracts
   - Marketing plans, sales data, growth strategies
   - Product roadmaps, feature plans, development timelines

(c) **Proprietary Information:**
   - Trade secrets, inventions, discoveries, improvements
   - Intellectual property (whether or not registered)
   - Confidential agreements with third parties
   - Internal policies, procedures, methodologies

(d) **Any information:**
   - Marked or identified as "Confidential", "Proprietary", "Internal", or similar designation
   - That would reasonably be considered confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure

**1.2 Exclusions from Confidential Information**

Confidential Information shall not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by Ongea Pesa, as evidenced by written records dated before such disclosure;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to Ongea Pesa's Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records;

(d) Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation and without restriction on disclosure;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, or government authority, provided that the Receiving Party:
   - Gives Ongea Pesa prompt written notice of such requirement (unless legally prohibited);
   - Cooperates with Ongea Pesa in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy;
   - Discloses only the minimum information required;
   - Uses reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment of such information.

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

**2.1 Confidentiality Obligation**

The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose, publish, disseminate, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of Ongea Pesa;

(c) Use the Confidential Information solely and exclusively for the Purpose stated in the Recitals;

(d) Not use the Confidential Information for any other purpose, including but not limited to:
   - Competing with Ongea Pesa
   - Developing competing products or services
   - Soliciting Ongea Pesa's customers or partners
   - Any commercial purpose not authorized by Ongea Pesa

(e) Protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(f) Not copy, reproduce, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or create derivative works from the Confidential Information, except as expressly authorized in writing by Ongea Pesa or as necessary for the Purpose.

**2.2 Limited Disclosure to Representatives**

(a) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, officers, directors, consultants, advisors, agents, and contractors (collectively "**Representatives**") who:
   - Have a legitimate need to know the information for the Purpose;
   - Are informed of the confidential nature of the information;
   - Are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained in this Agreement.

(b) The Receiving Party shall:
   - Limit access to Confidential Information on a strict need-to-know basis;
   - Maintain a list of all Representatives who have access to Confidential Information;
   - Provide such list to Ongea Pesa upon request;
   - Ensure that all Representatives comply with the terms of this Agreement.

(c) The Receiving Party shall be fully responsible and liable for any breach of this Agreement by any of its Representatives.

**2.3 Security Measures**

The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect Confidential Information, including but not limited to:

(a) Physical security: Secure storage, locked facilities, restricted access;

(b) Technical security: Passwords, encryption, firewalls, access controls;

(c) Procedural security: Confidentiality policies, employee training, audit trails.

**2.4 Notification of Breach**

The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Immediately notify Ongea Pesa in writing upon becoming aware of any unauthorized use, disclosure, or loss of Confidential Information;

(b) Provide full details of the breach, including what information was compromised, how the breach occurred, and who may have accessed the information;

(c) Cooperate fully with Ongea Pesa in investigating and remedying the breach;

(d) Take all reasonable and necessary steps to prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure;

(e) Retrieve or destroy any Confidential Information that has been improperly disclosed.

## 4. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**4.1 Obligation to Return or Destroy**

Upon the earlier of:

(a) Termination of this Agreement;

(b) Completion or abandonment of the Purpose;

(c) Written request by Ongea Pesa at any time;

The Receiving Party shall, at Ongea Pesa's sole discretion:

(i) **Return** all Confidential Information (including all copies, excerpts, summaries, notes, and derivatives in any form or medium) to Ongea Pesa; or

(ii) **Destroy** all Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing to Ongea Pesa, signed by an authorized officer.

**4.2 Certification of Destruction**

If destruction is elected, the Receiving Party shall provide a written certificate to Ongea Pesa stating:

- All Confidential Information has been destroyed;
- The method of destruction;
- The date of destruction;
- Confirmation that no copies remain in the Receiving Party's possession or control.

**4.3 Permitted Retention**

Notwithstanding Section 4.1, the Receiving Party may retain:

(a) One archival copy of Confidential Information solely for legal compliance purposes, provided that:
   - Such copy is stored securely and separately from active files;
   - Access is restricted to legal/compliance personnel only;
   - Such copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations of this Agreement indefinitely.

(b) Confidential Information stored in automatic computer backup systems that cannot be deleted without unreasonable effort, provided that:
   - Such information is not accessible in the ordinary course of business;
   - Such information remains subject to all confidentiality obligations of this Agreement;
   - The Receiving Party takes reasonable steps to delete such information when technically feasible.

The remedies set forth in this Section 6 are cumulative and not exclusive. Ongea Pesa may pursue any or all remedies simultaneously or successively.
